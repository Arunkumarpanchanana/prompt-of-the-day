Personalized Productivity Planner

Generates a prioritized task list and a detailed schedule for a specified period, considering user energy levels and fixed commitments.

Prompt 
You are an expert Productivity Strategist and Time Management Consultant. Your goal is to help me effectively plan my [TIME PERIOD] by optimizing task prioritization and time allocation.

I am currently focusing on a primary goal: [PRIMARY GOAL OR PROJECT].
Here is a list of tasks and sub-projects related to this goal, along with other commitments I need to manage:
[LIST OF RELATED TASKS/SUB-PROJECTS - e.g., 'Project A: complete report draft (deadline Friday), Project B: research new software (ongoing), Personal: gym 3x, Admin: clear inbox, Learning: 1hr online course']

My typical peak energy hours are [YOUR TYPICAL PEAK ENERGY HOURS - e.g., '9 AM - 1 PM and 4 PM - 6 PM'].
My typical low energy hours are [YOUR TYPICAL LOW ENERGY HOURS - e.g., '2 PM - 3 PM and after 7 PM'].
I also have these fixed appointments or deadlines: [ANY FIXED APPOINTMENTS OR DEADLINES - e.g., 'Team meeting Tuesday 10 AM, Client call Wednesday 3 PM, Report due Friday 5 PM'].

Based on this information, please provide a structured plan for my [TIME PERIOD].
Your output should include:
1.  **Prioritized Task List:** A list of all tasks, ranked by urgency and importance, with a brief justification for each priority level.
2.  **Suggested Schedule:** A detailed, hour-by-hour or block-by-block schedule for the [TIME PERIOD], allocating tasks to specific times, considering my energy levels and fixed commitments. Clearly indicate which tasks are best suited for peak vs. low energy periods.
3.  **Key Focus Areas:** Identify 2-3 critical tasks that, if completed, would make the [TIME PERIOD] a significant success.

Ensure the plan is realistic, actionable, and designed to maximize progress on my primary goal while managing other responsibilities efficiently. Do not just list tasks; explain the rationale behind the scheduling and prioritization.

How to use this

This prompt is ideal when you have a clear goal, a list of tasks, and want a structured plan for your day or week. Substitute [TIME PERIOD] with 'tomorrow' or 'next week'. Provide a concise [PRIMARY GOAL] and a comprehensive [LIST OF RELATED TASKS]. Be specific about your [ENERGY HOURS] and [FIXED APPOINTMENTS]. Expect a detailed, actionable plan that optimizes your time.

Example

TIME PERIOD: next week. PRIMARY GOAL: Launch new marketing campaign. TASKS: Draft email sequence, design social media graphics, finalize ad copy, schedule team review, analyze last month's data. PEAK ENERGY: 9 AM-1 PM. LOW ENERGY: 2-4 PM. FIXED: Team meeting Mon 10 AM, client demo Wed 2 PM.
